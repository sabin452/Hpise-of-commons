Choose Your Issue

Every law starts with a problem. What do you want to change?

📌
What is a Bill?
A Bill is a proposal for a new law (or a change to an existing one) that Members of Parliament debate in the House of Commons. If approved by both Houses and given Royal Assent, it becomes an Act of Parliament — the law of the land.
💡 Think big — climate, health, education, inequality, housing, transport…
💡 Use statistics or real examples to make your case stronger!
2

Draft the Title of Your Bill

Every Bill needs an official short title and long title.

📝
Official format for the long title:
"A Bill to [action verb] [topic] and for connected purposes."
Examples "A Bill to reduce single-use plastics in educational settings and for connected purposes."
"A Bill to require all secondary schools to provide mental health support and for connected purposes."
"A Bill to establish a national youth climate council and for connected purposes."

✦ Useful action verbs — click to copy

reduce prohibit establish require regulate protect amend promote
💡 This is the name people will use to refer to your law.
3

Write the Preamble

The formal introduction explaining why this Bill is necessary.

📖
The Preamble sets the scene. It explains the problem, provides context, and justifies why Parliament must act. It uses formal, passive, and impersonal language.

✦ Parliamentary phrases — click to insert

WHEREAS… RECOGNISING that ACKNOWLEDGING NOTING WITH CONCERN IT IS HEREBY DECLARED BE IT ENACTED
Example preamble WHEREAS the current provision of mental health support in secondary schools is inadequate; RECOGNISING that one in four young people in England will experience a mental health problem in any given year; ACKNOWLEDGING that early intervention significantly improves long-term outcomes; BE IT ENACTED by the King's most Excellent Majesty by and with the advice and consent of Parliament…
💡 Write in CAPITALS for the key words (WHEREAS, RECOGNISING…) and include at least one statistic.
4

Write Your Clauses

The numbered provisions — the actual rules of your Bill.

⚖️
Each clause is one numbered rule or obligation. Together, your clauses form the heart of your Bill. Write at least 3 clauses.

Prepare Your Opening Speech

You will speak for approximately 2 minutes to present your Bill.

🎙
An MP always opens their speech by addressing the Speaker and their fellow MPs. Your speech should be passionate but formal.
